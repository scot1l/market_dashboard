    Breadth Notes
    Tradeoffs and signal definitions used in the generated breadth dataset.
      Column Guide
      Column Meaning
      GradeTrend structure from EMA10, EMA20, and SMA50.A = clean uptrend, B = transition or mixed structure, C = clean downtrend.
      SetupAutomatic constructive scan from Grade, RS21, Group%, AmtZ, and ATRx50.A good setup is either a clean leader or an early rotation candidate that is not yet too stretched.
      1D / 5D / 20DRaw return windows.1D shows the latest push, 5D shows short swing momentum, 20D shows the monthly leg.
      ATR%14-session ATR as a percent of price.Higher means more movement and more risk per position unit.
      ATRx50Distance from the 50-session average in ATR units.0 to +2 is constructive, above +3 is often extended, below 0 means below medium-term trend.
      RS2121-session percentile of volatility-adjusted relative strength versus the benchmark ETF.80 to 100 = strong leadership, around 50 = neutral, 0 to 20 = lagging.
      Group%20-day return percentile inside the same group.It is a cross-sectional rank. High means this ETF is beating its peers, not just its benchmark.
      Amt20Average 20-session traded amount in CNY 100m.Use it as a liquidity floor. Bigger is easier to trade and more credible for follow-through.
      AmtZLatest turnover shock versus the last 20 sessions.Above 0 means expanding participation. Above +1 is notable. Below 0 means interest is fading.
      RS ChartBars show the last 20 values of rolling relative strength. The orange line is the smoother signal line. Above zero means relative outperformance versus the benchmark; below zero means underperformance.
      Best Combinations
      Best trend-following setupGrade A or strong B, RS21 above 80, Group% above 70, AmtZ above 0, ATRx50 between 0 and +2. This is the cleanest “leader with participation but not yet too stretched” profile.
      Best early-rotation setupGrade B improving toward A, RS21 between 60 and 80, Group% above 60, AmtZ above +1, ATRx50 between -1 and +1. This often catches a new theme before it looks obvious on the chart.
      Strong but chase-risk setupGrade A, RS21 above 90, Group% above 80, 5D strong, but ATRx50 above +3. The trend is strong, but the reward-to-risk is usually worse unless you wait for a pause.
      Weak rebound, not leadership5D turns positive, but Grade stays C or weak B, RS21 stays below 40, and the RS chart remains below zero. That is usually just a bounce inside a laggard.
      Avoid or de-prioritizeGrade C, RS21 below 20, Group% below 30, AmtZ negative, and ATRx50 below 0. This is the classic weak group / weak instrument combination.
